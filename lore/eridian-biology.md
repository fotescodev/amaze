# Eridian Biology

## Physical Description

- **Size**: About the size of a Labrador retriever (Ch ~10)
- **Mass**: 168 kilograms (Ch ~14)
- **Body plan**: Pentagonal symmetry — five legs radiating from a central carapace
- **Carapace**: Roughly pentagonal, ~18 inches across, half as thick. Made of oxidized minerals — hard, rock-like (Ch ~10)
- **Legs**: Five legs, each with a joint in the middle ("elbow"). Each ends in a hand with triangular fingers, three per hand. All five hands are identical — no "front" or "back" (Ch ~10)
- **No eyes**: Eridians have no eyes whatsoever. Their homeworld surface is pitch-dark; light never reaches the ground through their thick atmosphere (Ch ~13)
- **No mouth/face**: No visible mouth, face, or facial expressions. Food is consumed through an abdominal opening (Ch ~16)
- **Skin**: Brownish-black, rock-like texture. Bare on the legs; carapace covered by clothing (Ch ~10)
- **Clothing**: Wears a shirt-like garment with five armholes covering the carapace. Dull greenish-brown, thicker than human clothing. Has an open hole on top (like a neck hole) that is smaller than the carapace — put on by pulling downward (Ch ~10)
- **Formal/celebration clothing**: Smooth cloth underlayment with symmetrical rigid shapes (almost like armor). Top vent hole ringed with rough gems (faceted but low quality — "blotchy and discolored" but large, presumably sound great to sonar). Sleeves stop halfway down arms, ornamented at cuffs. Shoulders connected by loose braided cords. Gloves on all five hands (coarse burlap-like material). Limits movement but is ceremonial. (Ch ~25)
- **Work clothing**: Jumpsuit with tool-belt bandolier and satchel (Ch ~12-13)

## Internal Biology

Eridian physiology is fundamentally different from human biology:

- **Biological material**: Only a few kilograms of actual biological (living) material. The rest is inorganic structures built and maintained by single-celled worker organisms (Ch ~13)
- **Worker cells**: Single-celled organisms that travel through the bloodstream, building up or repairing the body as needed. They also manage digestion and service the brain. Analogous to bees building a hive — if bees were single-celled and the queen was intelligent (Ch ~13)
- **Brain**: Sits safely in the center of the carapace. Serviced by worker cells (Ch ~13)
- **Nerves**: Inorganic silicates transmitting light-based impulses (Ch ~13)
- **Bones**: Honeycombed metallic alloys (Ch ~13)
- **Blood**: Mostly liquid mercury. Two separate circulatory systems (Ch ~13):
  - **Ambient system**: Blood at 210 degrees Celsius. Acts as heat sink when muscles cool
  - **Hot system**: Blood kept at 305 degrees Celsius — hot enough to boil water even at Erid's air pressure
- **Muscles**: Inorganic, made of porous sponge-like material sealed in flexible sacs. Majority of the body's water is tied up in these sacs. Muscles expand via hot blood (boiling water inside) and contract via cold blood (Ch ~13)
- **In short: Eridians are steam-powered** (Ch ~13)
- **Carapace composition**: Made of oxidized minerals (Ch ~13)
- **Radiator organ**: Five slits at the top of the carapace allow outside ammonia air in and out to dump heat. At no point does air enter the bloodstream — this is purely for heat exchange (Ch ~13)
- **Self-contained biosphere**: Has both plant-like and animal-like cells internally. Oxygen to CO2, CO2 to oxygen, back and forth, always in balance. Body only needs energy via food and airflow to dump heat (Ch ~13)
- **Energy storage**: Sacs that hold ATP (the main energy-storage medium of DNA-based life). Eridians have so much ATP they evolved specialized storage beyond cells. They are "biological smelters" — pull oxygen off minerals to get metals (Ch ~13)
- **Hot blood sterilization**: The hot circulatory system is too hot for biological material to survive — useful for sterilizing incoming food of pathogens (Ch ~13)

## Senses

- **Primary sense: Echolocation / sonar vision** (Ch ~11)
  - Uses ambient sound waves to resolve environment (passive sonar, not active like bats)
  - Tiny receptors all over outer shell report back to brain, "like touch"
  - His whole body is a microphone — brain does serious processing on time differences between sound hitting different body parts
  - Can "see" through thin metal (aluminum plate is like a glass window to him)
  - Cannot perceive ink on paper or thin markings — needs physical relief/thickness
  - Cannot "see" in vacuum (uses technology like cameras/radar for space operations)
- **Hearing**: Perfect pitch, can remember and reproduce exact musical notes
- **No vision**: Never evolved eyes; Erid's surface is pitch-dark
- **Sound-based "reading"**: Eridian clocks and writing use raised/thick symbols (~1/8 inch relief) rather than flat ink

## Homeworld: Erid (40 Eridani system)

- **Star**: 40 Eridani (Eridians have their own name for it)
- **Distance from Earth**: 16 light-years
- **Planet classification**: Super-Earth
- **Mass**: 8.5 times Earth's mass
- **Diameter**: About twice Earth's diameter
- **Surface gravity**: 20.48 m/s^2 (roughly double Earth's 9.8) (Ch ~14)
- **Day length**: 5.1 hours (spins very fast) (Ch ~13)
- **Year length**: 198.8 Eridian days (one Eridian year ~ 42 Earth days) (Ch ~14)
- **Atmosphere**: 29 times Earth's atmospheric pressure. Pure ammonia-based (Ch ~10)
- **Temperature**: 210 degrees Celsius at the surface (Ch ~13)
- **Water**: 210C water is still liquid due to extreme pressure (Ch ~13)
- **Surface**: Pitch-dark — light never reaches the ground (Ch ~13)
- **Magnetic field**: At least 25 times stronger than Earth's. Combined with thick atmosphere, provides 100% radiation protection on the surface (Ch ~13)
- **Life**: Biosphere exists in total darkness (like deep ocean life on Earth). All life uses heavy metals extensively (Ch ~13)
- **No moon**: Planets that close to a star rarely have moons — gravitational tidal forces rip them out of orbit (Ch ~13)

## Diet

- Food consists of rocky/mineral-covered meat (Ch ~16)
- Tears off mineral exterior to expose meat underneath, shreds meat finely with claws
- Eating is done through abdominal opening — armor on abdomen splits, flesh opens, food is dropped inside. No chewing, no teeth, no moving parts visible (Ch ~16)
- Eating is considered gross/private in Eridian culture — done alone. "Social discomfort. No talk." (Ch ~16)
- Before eating, expels waste through the same abdominal opening (a gray blob) (Ch ~16)
- After eating, lies on back immobile — then falls asleep (Ch ~16)

## Sleep

- Not like human sleep — Eridians are legitimately paralyzed during sleep (Ch ~13)
- Hot blood system must be cooled to ambient levels for worker cells to service it. During this time, muscles cannot function and brain has no conscious function (Ch ~13)
- **A sleeping Eridian cannot wake up** — they sleep until their body is ready (Ch ~13)
- Sleep duration is unpredictable: minimum ~3.4 hours, maximum ~12 hours (12,265 to 42,928 Earth seconds) (Ch ~12)
- Cultural norm: Eridians watch each other sleep for safety. Dates back to primitive times. Rocky asks Grace to observe him sleeping — "Want want want" (Ch ~12)

## Reproduction

- Hermaphrodites (Ch ~16)
- Reproduce by laying eggs next to each other. "Stuff happens" between eggs; one absorbs the other, leaving one viable egg (Ch ~16)
- Egg hatches in one Eridian year (42 Earth days) (Ch ~16)
- Laying eggs together is the Eridian equivalent of sex. They mate for life (Ch ~16)

## Age and Lifespan

- **Rocky's age**: 291 Earth years (Ch ~14)
- **Average Eridian lifespan**: 689 Earth years (Ch ~14)
- Rocky was born around the time of George Washington — he's not even that old for his species (Ch ~14)

## Memory and Cognition

- Eridians appear to have photographic/eidetic memory — Rocky remembers every word told to him even once, hours earlier (Ch ~12)
- Extremely good at math — can convert units in the blink of an eye, never wrong on arithmetic (Ch ~12)
- Can multitask effectively — hold conversation while doing delicate machinery work simultaneously (Ch ~13)
- Primary sense is sound, so memory may be auditory-focused (Ch ~12)

## Radiation Vulnerability

- Eridians are extremely susceptible to radiation and never knew it existed before space travel (Ch ~13)
- Erid's magnetic field and thick atmosphere provide 100% radiation protection on the surface (Ch ~13)
- Rocky's crew of 23 died from radiation sickness in interstellar space. Rocky survived because his workshop was near the engines, surrounded by Astrophage fuel (which blocks all radiation) (Ch ~13)
- Eridian cells die from radiation exposure — resembles radiation sickness. "Many many cells die. Not infection. Not injury. No reason." (Ch ~12)
