# Eridian Language

## Communication Mechanism

- Eridians speak using **musical chords** — multiple notes played simultaneously (Ch ~10-11)
- Sound is produced internally — no visible mouth. Notes come from "somewhere inside him" (Ch ~11)
- Mechanism is analogous to whale song: air moved back and forth across internal vocal structures without expelling it (Ch ~11)
- Rocky can produce **at least five different notes simultaneously** — one chord can contain up to 5 tones (Ch ~11)
- Likely has **five sets of vocal cords** (or equivalent), matching the pentagonal body plan (five arms, five hands, five vocal pairs) (Ch ~11)
- Sounds are within human hearing range — some low, some high, but definitely audible (Ch ~10)

## Sound Properties

- Each "syllable" is a chord — a combination of tones played at the same time
- Words consist of one or more syllable-chords played in sequence
- The specific frequencies (notes) within each chord encode the word's identity
- There are harmonics and resonances, but the main frequency peaks define the word
- Grace describes it as "whale song" or "whale chords" — musical, multi-tonal (Ch ~10)

## Known Word Structures (Canon Examples)

- **"One"**: Two notes played simultaneously (single syllable, 2-tone chord) (Ch ~11)
- **"Two"**: Four distinct notes (single syllable, 4-tone chord) (Ch ~11)
- **"Three"**: Two syllables — first syllable has 2 notes, second has 5 notes (2+5 tones) (Ch ~11)
- **"Yes"**: A chord (learned via jazz hands gesture) (Ch ~11)
- **"No"**: A different chord (learned via fists tapped together) (Ch ~11)

## Grammar Rules

### Question Particle
- Rocky always ends a question with the word "question" — it functions as a question marker/particle (Ch ~12)
- Example: "You come from where, question?" / "Why, question?" / "Is allowed, question?"

### Word Order
- Generally follows English SVO (Subject-Verb-Object) order — Rocky adapted to Grace's word order early on (Ch ~12)
- Some Eridian grammar sneaks in (question particle at end, occasional inversions)
- Rocky picked up English word ordering because he realized Grace can't automatically remember things, so he works with Grace's system (Ch ~12)

### Tripling for Emphasis
- A tripled word means extreme emphasis — this emerged as an unspoken agreement between Grace and Rocky (Ch ~12)
- Examples: "Bad bad bad" = extreme distress. "Want want want" = desperate desire. "Happy happy happy" = extreme joy. "Smart smart smart" = extremely smart.

### Octave Shift for Emotion
- Dropping an octave (halving all frequencies) indicates deep sadness or grief (Ch ~12)
- When Rocky said something an octave lower than normal, Grace bumped it up an octave and the translation software could then parse it (Ch ~12)
- Example: "Original crew was twenty-three. Now is only me." — said in lowered octave indicating grief
- A quaver on the first note indicates surprise or being impressed (Ch ~13)
- Higher pitch than normal indicates excitement or joy (Ch ~27)
- Monotone = very serious intonation (Ch ~14)

### Other Grammar Patterns
- Reads information left to right (same as English — 1 in 4 chance coincidence) (Ch ~10)
- No articles ("I fix thing" not "I'll fix the thing")
- Simplified verb forms ("I not know" not "I don't know")
- "Understand" used as acknowledgment (like "okay" or "got it")
- "Amaze" used as an exclamation of wonder

## Vocabulary Development

- Grace and Rocky built vocabulary from **numbers first** (1-5, then base-6 counting), then physical units (time, length, mass), then scientific concepts, then general words (Ch ~10-12)
- After "several hours" they expanded to **"several thousand words"** (Ch ~11)
- Language growth is exponential: the more words you know, the easier it is to describe new ones (Ch ~11)
- Rocky memorizes every word instantly with apparent perfect recall. Grace uses a laptop with waveform analysis software and a spreadsheet dictionary (Ch ~11-12)
- Grace wrote translation software: takes audio-analysis output, identifies musical notes, looks up words in table. Real-time English translation on laptop screen (Ch ~11)
- Eventually Grace can understand Eridian fluently without any computer. Rocky is equally fluent in English comprehension (Ch ~30/ending)

## Number System

- **Base six** (because Eridians have 3 fingers per hand, use 2 hands to count = 6 digits) (Ch ~10)
- Written digits: "l" (0), "I" (1), "V" (2), "lambda" (3), "+" (4), "V" (5) (Ch ~10)
- No digits 6-9 — after 5 they carry over

## Time Unit

- One **Eridian second** = 2.366 Earth seconds (Ch ~10)
- Eridian day = 7,776 Eridian seconds (6^5 — a "metric" number in base 6) (Ch ~14)
- Eridian year = 198.8 Eridian days = ~42 Earth days (Ch ~14)

## Writing System

- Uses raised/thick symbols (~1/8 inch relief) because Eridians "read" via echolocation/touch, not sight (Ch ~10)
- Eridian clock: mechanical rotor-based display with tactile symbols visible through windows. Like an old-school odometer (Ch ~10)
- Writing is not ink-based — must have physical dimensionality to be perceived

## Proper Nouns

- Grace and Rocky cannot pronounce each other's proper nouns (different sound production systems) (Ch ~12)
- Rocky's actual name is a sequence of musical notes with no meaning in Eridian language — Grace calls him "Rocky" (Ch ~16)
- Grace's name "Grace" is an English word, so Rocky calls him the Eridian word for "grace" (Ch ~16)
- When one tells the other a name, the other picks or invents a word in their own language to represent it
