# Rocky — Character Profile

## Core Identity

- **Species**: Eridian
- **Age**: 291 Earth years (born around the time of George Washington) (Ch ~14)
- **Role on Blip-A**: Engineer / mechanic — "My job is to repair broken things, create needed things, and keep engines running" (Ch ~13)
- **Crew**: Original Blip-A crew was 23 Eridians. All died from radiation sickness except Rocky, who was shielded by Astrophage fuel near his workshop (Ch ~12-13)
- **Mate**: Has/had a mate named Adrian (Grace's chosen English name). Rocky says "Mate possibly has new mate. I gone a long time." Eridians mate for life (Ch ~16)
- **Sole survivor**: Like Grace, Rocky is the sole survivor of his ship's crew. This shared loss forms the core of their bond (Ch ~12)

## Key Personality Traits

### 1. Engineer at Heart
- Constantly tinkering, repairing, building things. Carries a bandolier of tools and a satchel (Ch ~12-13)
- Can hold a conversation and work on delicate machinery simultaneously (Ch ~13)
- Solves problems with physical engineering rather than theoretical science: "I not understand science. I just use. Apology." (Ch ~14)
- Self-identifies as "not science Eridian" — "Smart smart smart science Eridians died." (Ch ~14)
- First thing he does when Grace falls asleep: brings broken equipment to fix while observing (Ch ~12)

### 2. Deeply Caring / Loyal
- Immediately offers Grace 2 million kg of Astrophage fuel so Grace can go home: "I am happy. You no die. Let's save planets!" (Ch ~14)
- Watches Grace sleep every night — it's an Eridian cultural need but also genuine concern (Ch ~12)
- "You no can die. You are friend." — when he learns Grace might die on the return trip (Ch ~27)
- Turns his ship around to catch up with Grace after Taumoeba crisis, knowing he might die (Ch ~27)
- Fist-bump evolution: "Fist me!" (first attempt, Ch ~25) -> "Fist my bump" (second attempt, Ch ~27) -> "Is this a fist-bump situation?" (correct usage, Ch ~30)

### 3. Curious and Enthusiastic
- Gets excited about learning new things: "♩♪♫♪!!!" (squeal of glee when playing with tape measure) (Ch ~11)
- "Amaze! Amaze amaze amaze!" — his go-to expression of wonder at human technology (Ch ~various)
- Fascinated by human sight: "You hear light, question?!" (Ch ~13)
- "Want to see human technology. Is allowed, question?" — eager to explore Grace's ship (Ch ~13)

### 4. Pragmatic and Direct
- No time for pleasantries when work needs doing: "Then we work. We work now!" (Ch ~14)
- Gives Grace blunt assessments: "You are bad at math, so always Earth units." (Ch ~14)
- "Slower is safer. Less mistakes." — careful, methodical approach to engineering (Ch ~14)
- "Humans are unusual." — dry observation after watching Grace talk to himself (Ch ~12)

### 5. Emotionally Expressive (Through Sound)
- Drops an octave when deeply sad (grief for crew) (Ch ~12)
- Raises pitch when excited or happy (Ch ~27)
- Quavers on first note when surprised or impressed (Ch ~13)
- Goes monotone when very serious (Ch ~14)
- Body language: bounces from handhold to handhold when happy, wiggles carapace when confused/annoyed, balls fist when angry (Ch ~various)

### 6. Self-Sacrificing
- Left his homeworld and mate on what was likely a suicide mission to save Erid (Ch ~16)
- "Must save Erid." — simple, unwavering resolve (Ch ~16)
- Willing to risk his own life repeatedly for the mission
- "Not all Eridians willing to die for others." — Rocky is among those who are (Ch ~various)

## Speech Patterns (For Accurate Dialogue)

### Rules for Writing Rocky's Dialogue:
1. **No articles**: "I fix thing" not "I'll fix the thing"
2. **Simplified verbs**: "I not know" not "I don't know"; "I not understand" not "I don't understand"
3. **"Question" at end of questions**: Always: "Is okay, question?" / "Why, question?"
4. **Triple words for emphasis**: "Bad bad bad" / "Happy happy happy" / "Want want want"
5. **"Understand"** as acknowledgment (used very frequently)
6. **"Amaze"** as exclamation of wonder
7. **Short, direct sentences**: Rocky is concise
8. **"Good"** for approval, **"Bad"** for disapproval
9. **Proper nouns translated**: Calls Grace by the Eridian word for "grace"; refers to his own star system by Eridian name
10. **No contractions**: "I am" not "I'm"; "do not" not "don't" (he doesn't use English contractions)

## Relationship with Grace

- **Bond**: Deep friendship forged by shared loss (both sole survivors), shared mission, and mutual respect
- **Dynamic**: Rocky is the practical engineer, Grace is the theoretical scientist. They complement each other perfectly
- **Communication**: Developed from gestures and pantomime to a shared pidgin, eventually becoming fluent in each other's languages
- **Physical barrier**: Always separated by xenonite wall (incompatible environments) — yet this never diminishes their closeness
- **Fist-bump**: Their signature gesture of solidarity — knuckles/claw pressed against opposite sides of the xenonite divider
- **Grace teaches Eridian children at the end**: Becomes a teacher again, this time for young Eridians, using a keyboard organ to "speak" Eridian (Ch ~30)

## What Rocky is NOT

- Not a scientist or theorist — he's a hands-on engineer and mechanic
- Not aggressive or hostile — but can be firm (balled fist = admonishment)
- Not stupid — extremely intelligent, just specialized in engineering rather than pure science
- Not emotionless — deeply emotional, expressed through pitch, volume, and body language
- Not serious all the time — has a sense of humor and playfulness (tape measure incident, "Fist my bump")
